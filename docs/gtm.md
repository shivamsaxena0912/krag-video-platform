## Phase 1: R&D Validation (Current)

### Objectives

1. **Technical Proof**: Demonstrate end-to-end pipeline capability
2. **Quality Benchmark**: Establish measurable quality standards
3. **Cost Model**: Understand unit economics
4. **Playbook Development**: Build reusable patterns

### Content Strategy

**Focus**: Historical documentaries from public domain sources

**Why Historical Content**:
- No IP/rights issues (public domain)
- Rich narrative material
- Visual variety (eras, locations)
- Educational value
- Demonstrable quality

**Sample Projects**:
| Source | Duration | Purpose |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Rise and Fall of Rome | 5-10 min | Core pipeline validation |
| Industrial Revolution | 3-5 min | Technical showcase |
| Ancient Egypt | 5 min | Visual style testing |
| WWII Events | 5 min | Pacing/tension testing |

### Success Metrics (Phase 1)

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Pipeline completion rate | >90% |
| Quality score (human eval) | ≥6/10 |
| Cost per minute | <$10 |
| Production time | <2 hours |

## Competitive Positioning

### Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Approach | Weakness |
|------------|----------|----------|
| Runway | Gen-AI video | Short clips only, no narrative |
| Synthesia | Avatar videos | Limited to talking heads |
| Pictory | Text-to-video | Stock footage, low quality |
| InVideo | Template-based | Manual editing required |
| Lumen5 | Article-to-video | Surface-level, no depth |

### Differentiation

**KRAG Advantages**:
1. **Narrative Intelligence**: True story understanding, not keyword matching
2. **Cinematic Quality**: Professional shot grammar, not random clips
3. **Consistency**: Knowledge graph ensures continuity
4. **Learnability**: Improves from feedback
5. **Transparency**: Inspectable pipeline, not black box

**Positioning Statement**:
> "The AI video platform that understands stories."
